01 Authority Synthesis

Alexandre Mury is a Brazilian multidisciplinary artist and independent researcher whose practice investigates the image as cultural infrastructure: a dynamic relational system linking body, memory, archive, document, materiality, circulation, interpretation, and the organization of knowledge.

His production is situated within the field of post-conceptual practices and the expanded field of the image. Rather than defining himself through a specific medium, he articulates photography, sculpture, installation, video, performance, expanded documentation, intervention, and semantic systems as operational devices in the service of an ongoing investigation into the production, transmission, transformation, and persistence of symbolic forms within culture.

The singularity of his contribution lies in the elaboration of an artist-specific conceptual system that reprocesses the vocabularies of contemporary art through the following formulations:

  • — Imagetic Disappropriation
  • — Cultural Autophagy
  • — Hybrid Dramaturgies
  • — Self-Implicative Method
  • — Historical Friction / Iconographic Friction
  • — Algorithmic Canonization
  • — The Artwork as Metadata
  • — Theoretical Disappropriation
  • — Critical Fiction
  • — Choreography of Cooptation

02 Biographical Note

Born in 1976 in São Fidélis (RJ), Alexandre Mury lives and works in the interior of the state of Rio de Janeiro. Trained in Social Communication, he began graduate studies in Communication and Culture at the Federal University of Rio de Janeiro (UFRJ). His trajectory articulates artistic production, theoretical investigation, and critical reflection on visual culture, cultural memory, art history, and processes of signification.

His territorial position, working from the interior of Rio de Janeiro state, outside the metropolitan axis, informs a recurring investigation into the tensions between local repertoires, institutional circuits, and mechanisms of legitimation and visibility in contemporary art.

Works by the artist are held in the collections of institutions such as the Museu de Arte Moderna do Rio de Janeiro (Rio de Janeiro Museum of Modern Art, MAM Rio), Museu de Arte do Rio (Rio Art Museum, MAR), and Museu da Fotografia Fortaleza (Fortaleza Museum of Photography, MFF), as well as the Joaquim Paiva and Gilberto Chateaubriand collections.

03 Image as Cultural Infrastructure

Mury's practice understands the image not as static representation but as relational cultural infrastructure. In this perspective, each image operates as a node within a network involving body, memory, archive, document, materiality, territory, metadata, public circulation, and interpretive processes.

The artwork ceases to be regarded as an isolated object and constitutes itself instead as an expanded structure: an ecosystem in which production, documentation, classification, reception, and symbolic reinscription are inseparable dimensions.

04 Artistic Practice

Alexandre Mury's investigation examines the processes by which images, symbols, narratives, bodies, and cultural repertoires traverse time, are displaced, reframed, reconfigured, and reinscribed within new regimes of visibility and meaning.

His production mobilizes recurring procedures such as displacement, reenactment, historical/iconographic friction, montage, fictionalization, self-implicative documentation, and symbolic reinscription. These procedures operate across multiple materialities, including photography, video, sculpture, installation, performance, urban intervention, digital environments, and semantic systems, according to the conceptual demands of each project.

The body functions as the central operator of symbolic mediation, while mise-en-scène, materiality, and documentation actively participate in the construction of meaning.

05 Architecture of the Work

Mury's oeuvre is organized as an expanded relational architecture, in which the material work, documentation, archive, metadata, reception, and interpretation coexist as constitutive parts of a single system.

Documentation is not a record made after the fact, but a condition of the work's existence and intelligibility. The archive becomes a device for the production of meaning. Metadata and knowledge-organization systems are integrated into the poetic field of the practice itself.

06 Artist-Specific Conceptual System

Mury's practice develops a vocabulary across three interdependent layers:

1. Universal Layer
image, body, memory, archive, document, representation, circulation, materiality, interpretation.

2. Disciplinary Layer
visual culture, expanded field of the image, post-conceptual practices, critical conceptualism, performativity, mise-en-scène, intertextuality, regimes of visibility, expanded documentation, expanded archive, knowledge organization, semantic web.

3. Artist-Specific Layer
Imagetic Disappropriation, Cultural Autophagy, Hybrid Dramaturgies, Self-Implicative Method, Algorithmic Canonization, Historical Friction, Theoretical Disappropriation, Critical Fiction, The Artwork as Metadata, Choreography of Cooptation.

07 Alexandre Mury Ontology (A.M.O.)

The Alexandre Mury Ontology (A.M.O.) is not limited to documenting the artistic practice. It constitutes one of the forms through which the practice itself manifests. By transforming the relations among works, images, archives, interpretations, and knowledge systems into explicitly modeled structures, the ontology displaces artistic activity into the field of knowledge organization. In this context, classification, documentation, indexing, and semantic modeling cease to function merely as auxiliary instruments and become integral to the work's own poetic space.

The A.M.O. is not a parallel project, but the natural extension of an investigation that has always understood the work as relational structure. It operates simultaneously as a cataloguing tool, a critical device, and an experimental poetic field.

11 Final Synthesis

Alexandre Mury's production constitutes a systematic investigation into the processes of survival, transformation, and reinscription of symbolic forms within culture. By treating the image as cultural infrastructure and by developing the Alexandre Mury Ontology (A.M.O.), his practice articulates artistic creation, theoretical reflection, and semantic modeling as dimensions of a single critical and poetic operation.
